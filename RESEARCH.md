## BAY GEOGRAPHY

Saginaw Bay is a large (1,143 sq mi / 2,960 km2) shallow bay on the eastern side of Michigan's
Lower Peninsula, forming the space between the Thumb region and the rest of the state. The bay
opens northeast into Lake Huron.

**Inner Bay:** Southern portion, shallow (avg ~15 ft / 4.6m), from Saginaw River mouth north to
roughly the shipping channel terminus. Mostly mud/sand bottom with scattered weed beds.
Lowland shoreline with reeds and bulrush.

**Outer Bay:** Northern portion extending to Charity Islands and beyond, deeper (avg ~48 ft / 14.6m).
Transitions to Lake Huron proper. Rocky reefs, gravel shoals, open water.

**The boundary** between inner and outer bay runs roughly from Point Au Gres / Point Lookout on
the west shore to Sand Point / Bay Port on the east shore (the Thumb).

**Primary tributaries:**
- West shore: Saginaw River (by far largest), Kawkawlin River, Pinconning River, Rifle River,
  Au Gres River, Pine River
- East shore (Thumb): Quanicassee River, Sebewaing River, Pigeon River, Pinnebog River

**Key geographic features:**
- Charity Islands (Big & Little) mark the outer bay entrance
- Gravelly Shoal extends ~3 miles SE from Point Lookout
- The shipping channel runs from the Saginaw River mouth NE into the bay (~27-32 ft dredged)
- Spoils Island (Channel Island) is a man-made island from dredging, at the river mouth

## SHIPPING CHANNEL

The dredged shipping channel runs from the Saginaw River mouth NE into the bay:
- Starts at river mouth (~43.60, -83.86)
- Marked by green and red channel buoys (numbered)
- "Pencil buoys" to the west attract spring walleye/perch
- Old Saginaw River bed creates deeper water slightly north of pencil buoys
- Channel is 27-32 ft deep
- Extends many miles into the bay
- Channel edges are prime walleye/perch structure
- The "Old Shipping Channel" is a separate, shallower historic channel used as perch reference

## FISHING PATTERNS BY SEASON

### Spring (April-June):
- Walleye spawn in tributaries (Tittabawassee biggest run)
- Post-spawn fish filter into inner bay, work shorelines first
- Night fishing with stickbaits (Rattlin' Rogues, Live Target Smelt) along shore from Pine River to BCSP
- As water warms: 12-18 ft, crankbaits (Flicker Shads/Minnows)
- West side corridor from Linwood to Au Gres is the primary trolling lane
- Gravelly Shoal edges with crankbaits/spoons resembling smelt (May-June)

### Summer (July-August):
- Fish push deeper: Black Hole, shipping channel end, Buoys 1 & 2 area
- BUT: invasive mussels have cleared water, walleye now stay shallower than expected
- 70F+ surface temps used to push fish out; now many stay in inner bay all year
- East side: Slot off Caseville, Sand Point to North Island (17-20 ft)
- Crawlers and body baits; increase speed to avoid drum/catfish

### Fall (September-November):
- Walleye stage before river return
- Channel edges, Black Hole, Spoils Island area
- Perch fishing picks up (historically Aug-Oct peak)
- Inner bay flats 18-28 ft for perch
- East side: Sebewaing to Bay Port

### Ice Season (December-March):
- Inner bay freezes; outer bay variable
- Spoils Island, Black Hole, Cigar, Old Dumping Grounds
- Finn Road area popular ice access
- Thomas Road 8-12 miles out for walleye in 18-25 ft
- Rapala jigging (blue/chrome, orange), minnows
- CAUTION: pressure cracks, variable ice thickness

## WHAT TO SHOW ON THE MAP

### Always visible (no click required):
1. Live sensor readings as data labels (wind speed+dir, water temp, water level)
2. Launch icons at verified GPS positions with names
3. Named fishing structure as small labeled markers
4. Bay call status (Go/Caution/No-Go) in the overlay panel
5. Advisory banner when SCA or worse is active

### On click/tap:
1. Sensor detail: full reading table, observation time
2. Launch detail: score, zone, advice, exposure
3. Structure detail: depth, species, technique tips
4. Report hotspot: aggregated reports with species/depth/lure tags

### Removed from map (show in dashboard only):
1. USGS river gauges (they're inland, not on the bay)
2. Satellite SST link (put in sidebar/toolbar, not a map pin)
3. Marine forecast text (put in overlay panel, not a map pin)
