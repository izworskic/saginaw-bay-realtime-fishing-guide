Planning a Saginaw Bay Trip

Most Saginaw Bay fishing trips succeed or fail based on wind. A 7-day outlook lets you identify the best windows before they arrive. The key variables to watch are wind speed and direction, wave height, and the small-boat weather window. A string of light southwest days is ideal for walleye trolling on the west side. Calm periods with stable barometric pressure favor perch on the inner bay flats.

Water temperature trends also matter. A warming trend of 2 to 4 degrees over several days can trigger aggressive walleye feeding. Rapid cooling, especially in spring, can shut down activity quickly. The forecast page will incorporate temperature trajectory into its daily fishability projections.
